This section uses three dimensional maps to analyse two dimensions of the data set.

The first dimension is denoted using colour, and shows the density of a particular ethnic group.
(Darker shades of a particular colour represent greater density in a particular region, and vise-versa).

The second dimension is denoted using height, and shows the growth of particular ethnic
group as a percentage of the 2011 Census data. Individual maps of a few ethnic groups
will be shown.

The map labelled 'Comparison' is also displayed as a three dimensional map.

The dimension of colour shows which particular ancestral group is dominant in particular areas
around Sydney. The dimension of height shows the proportion of those born overseas
(Taller height implies a larger proportion of overseas born compared to those born in Australia).
