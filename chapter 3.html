This particular data set is used by both businesses and the Government.

Businesses:

Many businesses will set their businesses in areas that have a high density of a
particular ethnic population, offering products and services specific to the
dominant ethnicity of that area.

Example 1:

In the Western Sydney region (Parramatta/Harris Park) there are numerous Indian
restaurants, grocery and clothing shops within a small span to cater for the dense
population of Indians living there. This is evident on a smaller scale in the Blacktown
region, however, is growing.

Example 2:

In the Eastwood region, to cater for the dense Chinese population, there are numerous
Asian supermarkets. In addition, a wide range of advertisements on billboards around
the suburb, particularly in train stations and bus stops, target the local Chinese
population by having ads in Mandarin (or other local languages). Many cultural events
also run in this region (ie, Chinese New Year).




Government:

The local/state Government will work with government bodies such as Urban Growth
NSW to develop plans and implement housing projects in new areas
(mainly Western Sydney) to create greater connectedness amongst different ethnic groups.

For e.g. The Ponds/Schofields/Kellyville are suburbs which aim to cater for a diverse range
of ethnic populations. From Census data, these regions are home to a large population of
Indians, Chinese, Italians, Philippines, and South Africans. Hence, there is a mixed
population group instead of a of 'hotspot' a particular ethnic group.
