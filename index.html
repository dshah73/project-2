Introduction

The objective of this data set on ancestry, using Parent's place of birth,
is to gain a better understanding of a person's ethnic background.
The purpose of asking for Parent's birth place is because a greater
percentage of people are becoming unsure about their true origins
and ancestry.

The analysis on this data set will provide an understanding of how
different ethnic groups like to live, who they are surrounded by,
and how situations change with time.

Chapter 1 provides a detailed understanding of where different ethnic
populations tend to live around and in which areas they dominate in
terms of density. It shows the growth of ethnic groups in locations
around Sydney over a span of five years.

Chapter 2 has data taken from "G10_Country of Birth of Person by
Year of Arrival in Australia". This provides an extra component to
the story by presenting information and patterns on how ethnic
groups tend to move around Sydney based on how long they have
lived in the Country.

Chapter 3 presents information on how the data set is being used today
by both businesses and all stages of governments.

With this data set comparisons can be made to languages, religions, and age.
However, this would not contribute to the story that is been told on this website.
