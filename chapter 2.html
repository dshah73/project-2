This video shows the movement of different ethnic groups or ancestries
based on the year they arrived to Australia (from 1986-2016).

In many cases, ethnic groups will either 'converge' to a particular area
(known as a 'hotspot') or 'diverge' away from a particular area.
However, this is evident only a few or many years after immigrants have
arrived to Australia.

Indian Ancestry:

New Immigrants with an Indian ancestry will mainly settle around
the suburbs of Parramatta, Harris Park, Wenthworthville, Toongabbie
and Westmead. Collectively, these suburbs form a local
hotspot within Western Sydney.

After a few years, after the immigrants have become accustomed to
their new environment, they will begin to 'diverge' away from this
'hotspot' and live in other areas around Sydney.

This pattern is primarily due to:

-Changes in family structure:

As family grows in size, larger homes as well as privacy is needed).

-New residential developments:

Families will be inclined to move to
new locations such as developments in Western and South-West Sydney.

-Increase in wealth:

After years of working and saving, many immigrants
will have enough money to move to wealthier suburbs (e.g. Castle Hill,
Baulkham Hills, East Surburbs and new residential developments).

Chinese Ancestry:

Recent Immigrants with a Chinese ancestry have initially settled around
different areas of Sydney rather than a particular hotspot. However, lately,
there has been an increase in the number of immigrants living around
Kensington and Camperdown due to the increase in international Chinese
students living around UNSW and USYD.

It can be seen that Chinese immigrants living in Australia for more than
10 years are living in particular hotspots around Sydney, mainly
Eastwood/Epping, Burwood, and Hurtsville regions.

This pattern is primarily due to:

-Increase in wealth:

With greater buying power, older Chinese immigrants
are able to buy properties in competitive housing markets which form in
these hotspots.

-Reluctance to change:

With the increase in local Chinese businesses
(e.g. grocery and speciality shops) in these hotspots, immigrants who
settled in these areas many years ago will be relcutant to relocate.
With their properties continually increasing in value, it provides them
with another reason to stay.

-Lack of space in hotspots:

With competetive markets in hotspots, new
Chinese immigrants are forced to live in other areas in Sydney.
Slowly this is leading to the development of new hotspots around Sydney.

New Zealand Ancestry:

There is no pattern as such evident within New Zealand immigrants.
Their residency is distributed evenly around Sydney and no hotspots
are evident.

They tend not necessarily seek to be close to others of the same ancestry.
This could be a result of their ability to interact and belong within any
given community. Infact, similarities can be drawn to those with an
Australian ancestry.
